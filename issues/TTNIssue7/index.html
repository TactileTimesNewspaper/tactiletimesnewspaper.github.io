Section 1: Fun things Coming Up this Summer

 

== Braille Creative writing contest - Canadian National Institute for the Blind

Are you interested in creative writing? Would you like to get recognised for your work? The CNIB have just launched their creative writing contest, and this year it’s open to UK entries too.

The competition is now open to primary and secondary students in the UK, and you’ll need to write a short story, essay or poem in hard copy braille. There are lots of cash prizes!! So why not get writing?!

The deadline is the end of May so this is probably more for people without exams.

More info at https://www.victa.org.uk/victa-calendar/braille-creative-writing-contest/

P.S. If you write something, send it to us too, and have it published in the next edition of the TTN (contributions can be sent to: info@tactiletimes.org).

 

== The Tactile Times launches Accessible Quizlet Viewer!

Have you heard of Quizlet? If so, it's probably not in a good way as we've found it incredibly inaccessible.

The core functionality of Quizlet is to help you revise words using all sorts of methods which are great for sighted people, but what about blind people? Until now they've missed out, but today that is changing.

You can have a go making your favourite Quizlet set accessible at:

https://quizlet.tactiletimes.org

Your Quizlet will open in a fully-accessible table with correct language tagging - more details at the link above.

Please note that this is still in the testing phase, and we rely on a fairly complex system for importing new Quizlet sets and making them accessible, so there may be times when the service for adding new Quizlet sets is temporarily down. If this happens please bear with us as we work to get it back up and running as quickly as we can. And, by the way, please do send us any feedback too.

 

== Global Accessibility Awareness Day (GAAD)

Taking place on 18 May, and all the details are on this link: Home - GAAD (accessibility.day)

 

It was co-founded by Jennison Asuncion, who’s a bit of a legend in the blind tech accessibility world. There are lots of interesting events, all over the world. You’ll have to hunt through to find one you like.

You might be interested in the event Sight and Sound are doing in partnership with the BATA at 12:30 pm that day tackling accessibility of financial services. If so, you can register for that one at: https://us06web.zoom.us/webinar/register/WN_IoHWah69Q92JwTUJHeTvUw#/registration

== Metro Blind Sports (Athletics event)

This is an annual event, which takes place in Mile End Stadium in East London.

Date: Saturday 24 June, in London

 

This year VICTA are offering up to £50 travel expenses for young people who live outside London to take part.

 

== LOOKfest is back!

Lookfest also has a new venue (Humble Farm in Monmouthshire).

Dates: Friday 7th July 14:00 – Sunday 9th July at 12:00.

Simply the best Festival for VI and blind people, families, and children. The last Lookfest was in 2019, and we’ve been waiting for the next one ever since. So much fun!!

See all the details on this link: LOOKFest: Tickets on sale now! – LOOK – Supporting Visually Impaired Young People to Thrive (look-uk.org)

 

== Sight Village is back

Sight Village is back, and at a new venue (Eastside Rooms, 2 Woodcock Street, Birmingham, B7 4BL)

Dates: Monday 17th July 10:00 – 16:00, and Tuesday 18th July 9:30 – 15:00.

A great list of exhibitors, from: ‘UltraCane,’ to OrCam, to Sight and Sound, and VisionAid Technologies, and many more…

See all the details on this link: Sight Village Central - Queen Alexandra College (qac.ac.uk)

 

== New College Worcester (NCW) Summer Activity Break

Dates: Tuesday 25th July – Thursday 27th July

For 11 – 16 year olds who are blind or vision impaired. 2 nights and 3 days of activities including: football, swimming, paddleboarding, arts and crafts, cooking, tandem riding.

See all the details on this link: Summer Activity Break - New College Worcester (ncw.co.uk)
